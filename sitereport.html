Introduction

    I started learning HTML and CSS as a part of a Web Development course in the second half of my first year at the University of Northampton. It was my first encounter with Web development, as I had previously learned only object-oriented programming.

Work in progress

    Since the first week, I have started implementing the knowledge I have gained at lectures and have used it as a foundation for my web project. I have decided to look for a nice background picture first and then work my way around it with the rest of the page. Since the background colours were quite dark, I decided to go for the white buttons to make sure they were visible. I did have a look at several portfolio pages from around the web, looking for inspiration. There were many exciting designs, and I could see some features that I could use on my website in the future.

    I did encounter some issues with the grid, but these issues were caused mainly by a lack of experience from my side. But after some time, I managed to overcome the problems and made sure the grid was fully functional.

    While working on this project, I found many different ways to make the code work. And this makes CSS even more enjoyable.

    The website will be updated in the future as my knowledge of CSS and JS grows. I feel there is plenty of space for improvement, and I'm looking forward to future challenges.

Summary

    Overall I'm satisfied with my webpage, and I'm happy that all the theoretical knowledge from the lectures came in handy. The hints and tips from the lecturer gave me a chance to improve the webpage and bring some excellent features to it.
